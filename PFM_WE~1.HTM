Executive summary

  • Harpak-ULMA launched the VTC 840 WD (8 Jul): a full IP66 washdown VFFS aimed at US food plants, debuting at PACK EXPO International in November. ULMA also added UK/Ireland service capacity (6 Jul). The only material competitor product move of the period.

Why it matters: ULMA's US arm is attacking hygienic VFFS (dairy, frozen, protein; washdown also matters in pet food) with a utility-cost pitch — and North America is the fastest-growing FFS geography (+38% cumulative 2023–25, 16.1% of segment sales).

Regulatory · EU

PPWR in 34 days; BPA ban in 11

Why it matters: compliance pressure lands on pouch and flow-wrap customers now — mono-material and PCR-capable line arguments carry immediate weight across EU markets.

Implication: compliance readiness becomes a differentiator in every active pouch and flow-wrap conversation before 12 Aug.
Details
PPWR (Reg. EU 2025/40) applies in full from 12 Aug 2026: mandatory reuse systems, PFAS ban in food-contact packaging, "recyclable in principle" design requirement. Commission guidance published 30 Mar 2026. Separately, Reg. (EU) 2024/3190: from 20 Jul 2026 new single-use food-contact packaging with BPA coatings may no longer be placed on the EU market (fruit/vegetable/fish cans and outer-surface-only coatings: Jan 2028). Sources: European Commission, 30 Mar 2026 · ItaliaImballaggio, 1 May 2026

How to read this briefing

Tags, cadence, and what gets included

New this week

First reported within the rolling 7-day window, with a dated source. These are the items worth reacting to.

Watchlist / Update

A development on something previously reported — only the delta is stated. "No change" means we checked and nothing moved.

Background

Older or recirculating coverage (typically interpack/PACK EXPO cycle) included only where commercially relevant to PFM's technologies and markets. Trade-show news often republishes weeks after the event — publish dates can mislead.

Ground rules

Every claim carries a source link and publication date. Undated or single-source items are flagged, not presented as fact. Quiet weeks are reported as quiet — no padding.
